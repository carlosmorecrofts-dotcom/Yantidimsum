1. Executive Summary

Vision: To become Medan's #1 premium dim sum brand combining authentic Oriental flavors with local North Sumatran touches, available on all delivery platforms within 2 years.

DY Dimsum (Dimsum Yanti Lubis) is a premium dim sum culinary business based in Medan, North Sumatra, Indonesia. We serve high-quality handmade dim sum using fresh, carefully selected ingredients, blending traditional Cantonese recipes with innovative local Medan flavors.

Key Differentiators

  • Handmade Premium — Every dim sum is freshly made daily without preservatives
  • Medan-Oriental Fusion — Unique variants like Andaliman Dimsum, Arsik Siumai, and Medan Chili Hakau
  • Online-First Strategy — Focused delivery via GoFood, GrabFood, and ShopeeFood
  • Frozen Ready-to-Cook — Frozen product line for wider distribution
  • Halal Certified — MUI Halal certification for broader market reach

Target Market

Medan has a population of 2.4+ million with a strong culinary culture. Our primary targets:

  • Ages 18-45, middle to upper-middle class
  • Students and office workers active on food delivery apps
  • Families seeking quality snacks and meals
  • Food bloggers and culinary communities in Medan

3. SWOT Analysis

STRENGTHS

  • Handmade premium product, not mass-produced
  • Unique Medan-Oriental fusion flavors
  • Competitive pricing for premium quality
  • Owner has culinary passion and knowledge
  • Online-first strategy reduces overhead
  • Medan = culinary city, massive market
  • Halal certified, reaching majority market

WEAKNESSES

  • New brand, not yet established
  • Limited initial production capacity
  • Dependency on delivery platforms (commission fees)
  • Limited shelf life for fresh products
  • Market education needed for fusion variants
  • Limited initial budget for mass marketing

OPPORTUNITIES

  • Dim sum trend in Indonesia rising +35% YoY
  • High food delivery penetration in Medan
  • Frozen food market booming post-pandemic
  • Collaboration with Medan food influencers
  • Expansion to neighboring cities
  • Corporate and event catering
  • Franchise model in year 3

THREATS

  • Existing dim sum competitors in Medan
  • Raw material price increases
  • Delivery platform policy changes
  • Cheap factory-made frozen dim sum
  • Fast-changing food trends
  • Food safety risks if uncontrolled
